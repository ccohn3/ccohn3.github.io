Are the Diamondbacks the most mediocre team in Major League Baseball to make the World Series?

Baseball
Diamondbacks

Author

Camden Cohn

Published

November 9, 2023

Throughout the Major League Baseball season, fans could tell that there were a few teams that stood above the rest. The Dodgers, Braves, Orioles and Rays had fantastic seasons and looked to be World Series favorites. But all of these favorites were upset by either the Texas Rangers or the Arizona Diamondbacks enroute to the World Series.

Were the Diamondbacks even a good team during the regular season? What starpower did they have besides a speedy rookie named Corbin Carroll?

Were the D-Backs the most mediocre team to make the playoffs this year? Let’s see what the numbers say.

To start, here are the teams with the best records from the regular season.

Code 
library(tidyverse)
library(ggrepel)
library(ggalt)
library(ggtext)
library(patchwork)
library(rvest)

bat <- read_csv("mlbbatting.csv")
pitch <- read_csv("mlbpitching.csv")
mlbscores <- read_csv("mlbgamescores23.csv")

totalstats <- bat |>
  inner_join(pitch, by=c("Tm" = "Tm")) 

differential <- totalstats |>
  mutate(
    RunDifferential = R.x - R.y
  ) |>
filter(Tm != "NA")|>
  filter(Tm != "League Average")

nlplayoff <- c("Milwaukee Brewers", "Atlanta Braves","Los Angeles Dodgers","Philadelphia Phillies","Miami Marlins")

teamlabels <- c("Milwaukee Brewers","Atlanta Braves","Los Angeles Dodgers","Philadelphia Phillies","Miami Marlins","Arizona Diamondbacks","Oakland Athletics","Texas Rangers")

nl <- differential |>
  filter(Tm %in% nlplayoff)

newlabels <- differential |>
  filter(Tm %in% teamlabels)

oak <- differential |>
  filter(Tm == "Oakland Athletics")

tex <- differential |>
  filter(Tm == "Texas Rangers")

hr <- bat |>
  filter(HR < 308)

newpitch <- pitch |>
  filter(Tm != "League Average")

nlfilter <- newpitch |> filter(Tm %in% nlplayoff) |> mutate(Playoff = "NL Playoff Team") |> select(Tm, Playoff)

nlpitch <- newpitch |> left_join(nlfilter)

dbpitch <- newpitch |> filter(Tm == "Arizona Diamondbacks")

nyy <- differential |>
  filter(Tm == "New York Yankees")

dbacks <- differential |>
  filter(Tm == "Arizona Diamondbacks")

records <- mlbscores |>
  group_by(Tm) |>
  summarise(
    TotalWins = max(Wins),
    TotalLosses = max(Losses)
  ) |>
  arrange(desc(TotalWins))

 ariwins <- records |>
  filter (Tm == "ARI") 

texwins <- records |>
  filter(Tm == "TEX")

ggplot() + 
  geom_dumbbell(
    data=records, 
    aes(y=reorder(Tm, TotalWins), x=TotalWins, xend=TotalLosses),
    size = 2,
    colour = "grey",
    colour_x = "blue",
    colour_xend = "orange") +
    geom_dumbbell(
    data=ariwins, 
    aes(y=reorder(Tm, TotalWins), x=TotalWins, xend=TotalLosses),
    size = 2,
    colour = "maroon",
    colour_x = "blue",
    colour_xend = "orange") +
    geom_dumbbell(
    data=texwins, 
    aes(y=reorder(Tm, TotalWins), x=TotalWins, xend=TotalLosses),
    size = 2,
    colour = "red",
    colour_x = "blue",
    colour_xend = "orange") +
  labs(
    x="", 
    y="", 
    title="Dbacks: worst record out of the teams that made the MLB playoffs", 
    subtitle="Arizona had 84 <span style = 'color:blue;'>Wins</span>, and 78 <span style = 'color:orange;'>losses</span> last season.", 
    caption="Source: Sports Reference | By Camden Cohn"
    ) +
  theme_minimal()+
 theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 14, face = "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 8), 
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank(),
    plot.title.position = "plot",
    plot.subtitle = element_textbox_simple()
  )

The Diamondbacks finished six games over .500 on the season, tied for the worst mark out of the teams that made the playoffs this past season. Did Arizona just experience a losing streak in the middle of the season or were they really just a middle of the pack team?

A couple of statistics that really show how dominant a team was throughout the season are runs scored per game, runs allowed per game and the run differential. Were the Diamondbacks actually a force in the National League this season?

The D-Backs allowed more runs per game than the MLB average and they scored fewer runs per game than the MLB average. They are the most mediocre team in Major League Baseball according to this bubble chart. They also had a negative run differential on the season at -15. Arizona allowed the most runs per game of any of the National League playoff teams and scored fewer runs per game than both the Dodgers and the Phillies, teams that they ended up beating in the playoffs.

Another couple of stats that often show team success are home runs and strikeouts. Where do the Diamondbacks rank in the league compared to other teams?

Code 
 bar1 <- ggplot() +

    caption = "Source: Sports Reference | By Camden Cohn"

Arizona was in the bottom third of the league in total home runs, just behind the Marlins and ahead of the Brewers but they were also in the bottom of the league in strikeouts thrown. Behind all of the other National League playoff teams and coincidentally right behind the other World Series team, the Rangers.

The Diamondbacks return a lot of players from this past year’s team and will look to improve on their first World Series appearance since 2001. As you can see, there’s plenty of room for improvement next season.
